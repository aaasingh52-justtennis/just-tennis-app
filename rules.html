Regular Season - How It Works

Match Format

Players must agree on format before the match begins.

Format Options:

  • One set
  • Best of 3 sets
  • Best of 5 sets

In Best of 3 or 5: optional 10-point tie-break (win by 2) can replace final set if both agree.

Scoring & Points System

Event Points
Match participation 1 point
Each set won 1 point
Match victory bonus 2 points
Maximum per match 5 points

Rationale: Ensures fairness between different formats. Best of 5 match wins are capped to avoid imbalance.

Max Number of Matches Between the Same Pair

  • Players may play the same opponent more than 3 times during the regular season, however only the first 3 reported matches between the same pair will count towards ranking points
  • Any matches beyond the 3rd encounter between the same players will not count towards ranking points

Ladder Rankings

Your ladder position is determined by total points earned. For players on the same number of points, rankings are tiebroken in this order:

  1. Match win/loss percentage
  2. Set win/loss percentage (only if match W/L% is equal)
  3. Order of joining the league (only if set W/L% is also equal)

The top 16 available players by ladder rankings qualify for playoffs (see Playoffs section for availability requirements).

Match Reporting

  1. All match results should be reported in the WhatsApp group
  2. At minimum, report the number of sets each player won
  3. Exact scores are optional but encouraged
  4. Either player may post the result; it must be acknowledged by the opponent (e.g. thumbs up)
  5. Use your assigned Player ID when reporting results (found in the contact list and ladder postings)

Arranging Matches

Court Booking

  • Players organize court booking themselves
  • Contact details, locations, and player levels are available in the shared contact sheet

Finding Opponents

  • Players can arrange matches with whoever they want
  • Use the excel sheet to filter by location zones, skill levels, budget and timings to find suitable opponents
  • Players can post in the group with specific court bookings/times/locations (be clear about levels and fees)
  • Players can directly contact others, but observe proper etiquette (don't message too late at night unless you know the person)

Mid-Match Format Changes

If match duration or court availability is limited, players may modify the format mid-match by mutual agreement.

Examples:

  • Switch to a 10-point tie-break instead of a full deciding set
  • Start a 2nd/3rd set at 2-2 instead of 0-0

Injury Policy

If a player is injured mid-match and cannot continue, the uninjured player is awarded all remaining sets.

Example: In a best of 3 match, if Player A wins the first set but gets injured in the second set and cannot continue, Player B is awarded the second and third sets.

Scoring:

  • Injured player gets points for participation (1) plus any sets won before injury
  • Uninjured player gets full match points as if they won all remaining sets

Late Arrival / Late Cancellation

If a player is 20+ minutes late to a scheduled match OR cancels within 3 hours of the scheduled start time, the opponent has the right to enforce a walkover (though they may choose to show grace and reschedule).

Walkover Scoring:

  • Present player receives 5 points
  • Absent player receives 0 points
Important: Players who commit 2 such infractions (late arrival/short notice cancellation) will be removed from the league.

MVP Award

MVP Nominees

At the end of the regular season, MVP nominees will be the 3 players who have played the most regular season matches. If there is a tie for the 3rd spot (or higher), there can be more than 3 nominees.

Eligibility

If you were MVP in any of the previous 3 seasons, you are not eligible for MVP again. This is to share recognition across the community rather than the same players winning repeatedly.

Voting Process

All players on the ladder will have the opportunity to vote for their MVP via a Google Form at the end of the regular season.

Voting Criteria

The MVP award is about community contribution, not just tennis ability.

When voting, consider how the nominee adds value to the Just Tennis community through:

  • Growing the community: Inviting friends, sharing the group link
  • Facilitating matches: Sharing court bookings with others, helping organize games
  • Inclusivity: Playing with people of different skill levels and backgrounds
  • Organizing friendly games: Taking initiative to bring players together
  • Sportsmanship: Fair play, positive attitude, encouraging opponents
  • Community spirit: Supporting other players, creating a welcoming atmosphere

The MVP embodies what Just Tennis is about: building an inclusive, active, and supportive tennis community where everyone feels welcome.

Award

The MVP award is funded by optional 20 AED donations during the regular season. The winner receives recognition and a small prize funded by these contributions.

Playoffs

Playoff Format

The Top 16 available players by ladder rankings qualify for playoffs.

Availability Requirement: Playoff seedings are finalized on April 2 (buffer day) after confirming player availability. If any players in the top 16 by ladder rankings cannot participate, all players below them move up one position in the seedings. The next available player(s) from the ladder fill the remaining spots (becoming the new 16th seed, and so on if needed). This ensures no walkovers in the first round and maintains proper seeding order.

Playoff Match Format

Players are encouraged to book 2 hours for playoff matches where possible.

  • 2-hour booking: Either player may request Best of 3, which becomes the default format. The other player cannot refuse.
  • 1-hour booking: Single set by necessity.
  • Compromise option: Players may agree to an extended set (first to 10 games, win by 2) as a middle ground.
  • If booking length is ambiguous or disputed: Spin a racquet ‚Äî winner chooses format. (This cannot override the 2-hour Bo3 rule when booking is clearly 2 hours.)
  • Running out of time: If a Bo3 match runs long, the 3rd set may be replaced with a 10-point tiebreaker (win by 2) by mutual agreement.
  • Final: Must be Best of 3 sets (no exceptions).

16-Player Format with Split Bracket Redemption

Round of 16 ‚Äî Two Separate Brackets:

Seeds are split into a Top Bracket (1-8) and Bottom Bracket (9-16).

  • Top Bracket winners ‚Üí Advance directly to Quarterfinals
  • Top Bracket losers ‚Üí Proceed to Redemption Round
  • Bottom Bracket winners ‚Üí Proceed to Redemption Round
  • Bottom Bracket losers ‚Üí Eliminated immediately

Key advantage: The entire bracket is predetermined from day 1. No re-seeding calculations needed, and no rematches until Semifinals.

Court Booking

Since playoff matchups are mandatory, the following court booking rules apply:

  • If either player cannot afford or does not wish to pay for private court fees, the match must be played at free public park courts (or free residential courts if either player has access to one)
  • If one player prefers a paid facility while the other wants free courts, the match shall be played at a free public park or residential court unless the player requesting the paid facility agrees to cover both players' court fees
  • This ensures playoffs remain accessible regardless of budget

Timing Flexibility

While deadlines must be met, players have flexibility within each round's window to schedule their specific match time. Quarterfinals, Semifinals, and Finals offer more time per match than earlier rounds.

Important: Round of 16 (April 3-8) and Redemption Round (April 9-12) are both non-negotiable. If you cannot play during these windows, do not enter the playoffs.

Prize & Transfer System

Entry Fee & Prize Distribution

Playoff participation requires a 30 AED contribution from your own pocket ‚Äî that's all you ever put in.

Note: The optional 20 AED MVP donation during regular season does not affect playoff qualification.

  • Everyone contributes 30 AED (their own money)
  • ü•á Winner receives: 320 AED (+290 net)
  • ü•à Runner-up receives: 160 AED (+130 net)
  • Everyone else: -30 AED (14 players √ó 30 AED each = -420)
  • Net total: +290 + 130 - 420 = 0 (zero-sum)
  • Total prize pool: 480 AED (16 players √ó 30 AED)

The knockout transfer system described below achieves exactly this distribution ‚Äî no upfront collection needed, and transfers happen naturally as players are eliminated.

How the Knockout Transfer System Works

By entering the playoffs, you agree to the following knockout transfer structure:

When you are knocked out of the playoffs, you must transfer to the player who eliminated you:

  • Your own 30 AED contribution (the only money from your own pocket)
  • Plus any funds you're holding (money collected from opponents you previously knocked out ‚Äî this isn't your money, you're just passing it forward)

Key point: You only ever put in 30 AED of your own money. Everything beyond that is prize pool money flowing through you to the eventual winner.

Note: "Collected" money is funds you received from players you knocked out. It's not your money ‚Äî it's prize pool money you're holding temporarily until you're eliminated (or win).

Transfer Rules

  1. Transfer must be completed within 48 hours of being knocked out (Note: For Top Bracket R16 losers, you're only knocked out after losing the Redemption round, not after your initial R16 loss. Bottom Bracket losers and QF onwards: losing = knocked out immediately.)
  2. Walkovers/Forfeits: If a player forfeits or no-shows a playoff match (cannot make the deadline, etc.), they are considered knocked out and must still transfer: 30 AED (own contribution) + any collected funds they hold.
  3. Confirmation: A pinned message will be posted by the organizer in the group. Players who are knocked out must thumbs up the pinned message as soon as they have completed their transfer
  4. Transfer method: Direct bank transfer or cash (arranged between players)
‚ö° R16 & Redemption: Transfer Immediately
For Round of 16 and Redemption rounds, players are encouraged to transfer as soon as possible after being knocked out ‚Äî ideally within a few hours. The tight schedule between rounds means your opponent may need those "collected" funds to pass forward if they lose their next match. The 48-hour rule is the hard deadline, but faster is better for prize pool liquidity.

Non-Transfer Consequences

Failure to transfer within 48 hours of being knocked out results in:

  • ‚ùå Immediate disqualification from all future Just Tennis seasons until transfer is made
  • ‚ùå Name posted in group as "transfer outstanding"
  • ‚ùå No exceptions

The organizer will not act as middleman. Transfer responsibility is between players directly.
